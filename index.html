Information Flow Analysis in LLM Reasoning

2025.06 - Present

Fuzhou University

Investigating how large language models process and propagate information during multi-step reasoning tasks.

  • Proposed Step-Saliency, a method that maps token-level saliency scores to step-level reasoning flow, enabling fine-grained diagnosis of reasoning failures
  • Developed StepFlow, a training-free framework that identifies "shallow-layer lockup" and "deep-layer decay" failure modes for early prediction of reasoning breakdowns
  • Achieved significant improvements: R1-Distill-32B +11.8 on AIME25, GPT-OSS-20B +9.5 on LiveCodeBench

Latent Space Geometry in Vision-Language Models

2025.03 - 2025.12

Fuzhou University

Analyzing geometric properties of LVLM hidden representations to understand and mitigate hallucination.

  • Led research on latent space geometry analysis, proposed "angular dispersion" metric to characterize the depth-wise transition from near-linear to high-curvature manifolds
  • Designed MAGE (Manifold-guided Geometric Enhancement), an adaptive layer-wise intervention strategy based on visual token projection and curvature estimation
  • Achieved SOTA on hallucination benchmarks: CHAIR sentence/instance level reduced from 48.0%/13.9% to 37.8%/11.2%, MME score improved from 600.2 to 676.3
